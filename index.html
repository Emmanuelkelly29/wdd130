Ogun

Ogun State, in southwestern Nigeria, shares borders with Lagos, Oyo, Osun, Ondo, and the Republic of Benin. Covering about 16,762 square kilometers, it is home to over 5 million people, with significant urban centers like Abeokuta, Sagamu, and Ijebu Ode. As a major industrial hub, Ogun hosts many manufacturing companies, particularly in Agbara and Ota, and is known for its agricultural production, including kola nuts, cocoa, and palm oil. Its cultural heritage is rich, with historical sites like Olumo Rock and its vibrant festivals drawing visitors.
